The Sanctuary

Sacred Spaces for
Every Season of Life

From weekend retreats to final passage,
Alexandria offers places of profound peace.

The sanctuary is not a building—it is a landscape. Nestled on the slopes of Palgongsan mountain, our spaces emerge from the terrain itself: caves carved by millennia of wind and water, gardens that follow the natural contours, and buildings that whisper rather than shout.

Whether you come for three days or three years, the mountain will be your teacher. The silence will speak what words cannot.

Three Dwelling Places

Choose Your Path

Pillar I

Cave Retreat

동굴수행 · Donggul Suhaeng

Enter the womb of the mountain. Our natural caves have sheltered contemplatives for centuries. In total darkness and silence, the mind turns inward. What remains when all distraction falls away?

  • 3-day, 7-day, and 21-day silent retreats
  • Natural cave chambers heated by geothermal warmth
  • Guidance from experienced meditation teachers
  • Integration sessions to bring insights into daily life
Explore Cave Retreats
Pillar II

Contemplative Residence

명상의 집 · Myeongsang-ui Jip

For those seeking extended immersion, our residency program offers a home within the sanctuary. Live alongside fellow seekers, participate in daily practice, and weave contemplation into the fabric of ordinary life.

  • 3-month, 6-month, and 1-year residencies available
  • Private quarters with mountain views
  • Shared meals, work practice, and communal gatherings
  • Access to library, gardens, and all sanctuary programs
Learn About Residency
Pillar III

Hospice Care

돌봄의 집 · Dolbom-ui Jip

When the final journey approaches, Alexandria offers a place to die with dignity, surrounded by beauty and love. Our hospice integrates the highest standards of medical care with deep spiritual support.

  • 24-hour palliative care by trained nurses
  • Spiritual companions from all traditions
  • Private suites with gardens and mountain views
  • Family accommodation and bereavement support

Our Location

Palgongsan

The "Mountain of Eight Merits" has been sacred ground for over 1,500 years. Its caves sheltered the first Buddhist monks to arrive in Korea. Its slopes hold dozens of temples and hermitages. The mountain does not merely host Alexandria—it teaches us.

From our sanctuary, you can walk to ancient temple sites, meditate by waterfalls, or simply sit and watch the mist rise through the pines. The mountain is not separate from the practice—it is the practice.
