Seven Gates of
Inner Passage

Each gate names a threshold the soul crosses — in illness, in contemplation, in the final hours. Alexandria accompanies every passage, every tradition, every language of the spirit.

  • I

    The Fracture of Mind

    Where the map of the self dissolves — accompaniment through psychosis, dementia, and the bewildering corridors of cognitive unraveling.

    →
  • II

    The Dissolution of Self

    At the threshold of identity — where consciousness questions its own edges, and the seeker stands between who they were and who they are becoming.

    →
  • III

    The Touch of God

    The encounter with grace — explored across Protestant devotion, Catholic mysticism, Islamic surrender, Buddhist awakening, and Hindu darshan.

    →
  • IV

    Inside and Outside

    The tension between institution and enlightenment — how religion shelters and constrains, and where authentic dialogue transcends every wall.

    →
  • V

    Sound and Soul

    The sacred acoustics of the inner life — Gregorian chant, Dhikr, Kirtan, temple bells: sound as the oldest bridge between the human and the holy.

    →
  • VI

    The Forbidden Gate

    At the edge of sanctioned experience — holotropic breathwork, cave retreat, and the consciousness territory that every tradition both fears and reveres.

    →
  • VII

    The Final Gate

    The art of dying well — residential accompaniment, the final passage, and the ancient wisdom every tradition holds for those who stand at the threshold of departure.

Cross-Faith Framework

Two Lines,
One Inquiry

Alexandria holds two great rivers of spiritual understanding in sustained conversation — not to judge or convert, but to illuminate the common human need flowing through every tradition.

Abrahamic Line

Indic Line

Islam runs parallel to Protestant Christianity as the third branch of the Abrahamic baseline — each carrying forward the covenant of the one God through its own complete and irreducible revelation.

Hinduism is the deep root from which Buddhism grew: the Dharmic soil of karma, rebirth, and liberation shaped the very questions the Buddha carried into the forest. Alexandria honors both the root and the flowering branch.

All traditions welcome  ·  No hierarchy  ·  No conversion  ·  Only accompaniment

Our Foundation

Three Commitments

01

No Faith Imposed

We receive every guest exactly where they stand — believer, doubter, seeker, or none. No prayer is required. No doctrine is taught. The sanctuary is yours to fill with whatever meaning you bring to its threshold.

02

Every Door Open

Protestant, Catholic, Muslim, Buddhist, Hindu, or walking an unnamed path — every tradition is held with the same care. The companions of Alexandria speak many spiritual languages, and listen in all of them.

03

All Gates Valid

The Seven Gates are not a ranking. There is no higher or lower passage. The fracture of mind is as sacred as the final breath. Every threshold Alexandria accompanies carries the same gravity and the same grace.
